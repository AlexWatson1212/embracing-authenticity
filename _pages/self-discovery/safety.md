<section class="start-page">

  <div class="start-intro">
    <p class="lead">
      Calm first. Insight second.
    </p>

    <p>
      If your nervous system feels threatened, everything feels harder — even good advice.
      Safety is the foundation of the whole self-discovery journey.
    </p>

    <p>
      This is not about pretending everything is fine. It is about helping your body learn:
      <em>I am not in danger right now.</em>
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="start-soft-block">
    <p class="home-kicker">Step 1</p>
    <h2>What safety actually means</h2>

    <p>
      Safety can be emotional, physical, relational, or environmental.
      Sometimes it is a locked door. Sometimes it is saying no.
      Sometimes it is food, sleep, quiet, or walking away from too much stimulation.
    </p>

    <p>
      Healing becomes much harder when your body is constantly braced.
      Safety gives you enough room to feel, think, choose, and repair.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="start-grid">
    <article class="start-card start-card--primary">
      <p class="home-card-step">Practice</p>
      <h3>The 3-part reset</h3>
      <p>
        1. Body: water, food, breath.<br>
        2. Space: tidy one small area.<br>
        3. Contact: send one safe message or speak kindly to yourself.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="start-card">
      <p class="home-card-step">Reminder</p>
      <h3>Low-demand days count</h3>
      <p>
        Some days your job is to do less. Eat. Rest. Walk. Keep things simple.
        A low-demand day can be a wise day.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="start-card">
      <p class="home-card-step">Boundary</p>
      <h3>Protect your peace</h3>
      <p>
        Boundaries are not aggression. They are structure for safety.
        A quiet “that doesn’t work for me” can be enough.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="start-card">
      <p class="home-card-step">Question</p>
      <h3>What would make today easier?</h3>
      <p>
        Ask yourself: “What would make today 10% easier?”
        Then do that one thing.
      </p>
    </article>
  </div>

  <div class="start-guidance">
    <h2>Before you move on</h2>
    <p>
      Do not rush into deep emotional work if your body is already overwhelmed.
      Start with safety. Return to safety. Build from safety.
    </p>

    <p>
      When you feel steady enough, continue to the next step:
      <a href="/self-discovery/reparenting/" class="inline-link">Reparenting →</a>
    </p>
  </div>
