<section class="start-page">

  <div class="start-intro">
    <p class="lead">
      Reparenting is not becoming perfect.
    </p>

    <p>
      It is learning how to stay with yourself in moments when you would once have abandoned yourself.
    </p>

    <p>
      Many of us learned to criticise ourselves, rush ourselves, ignore our needs, or push through pain.
      Reparenting is the slow process of building a different relationship with yourself.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="start-soft-block">
    <p class="home-kicker">Step 2</p>

    <h2>What reparenting actually means</h2>

    <p>
      Reparenting is not pretending your childhood was worse than it was.
      It is recognising that some needs were not consistently met and learning how to meet them now.
    </p>

    <p>
      It means becoming the calm, reliable adult that your younger parts can gradually learn to trust.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="start-grid">

    <article class="start-card start-card--primary">
      <p class="home-card-step">Need</p>
      <h3>Comfort</h3>

      <p>
        Sometimes healing begins with something surprisingly simple:
        tea, warmth, rest, a blanket, a favourite song, or sitting quietly with yourself.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="start-card">
      <p class="home-card-step">Need</p>
      <h3>Protection</h3>

      <p>
        Reparenting sometimes means saying:
        “No. That isn't okay.”
        Boundaries are a form of care.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="start-card">
      <p class="home-card-step">Need</p>
      <h3>Encouragement</h3>

      <p>
        Many people received criticism far more often than encouragement.
        Speak to yourself as you would a child who is genuinely trying.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="start-card">
      <p class="home-card-step">Need</p>
      <h3>Presence</h3>

      <p>
        Not every feeling needs solving.
        Sometimes your younger self simply needs somebody to stay.
      </p>
    </article>

  </div>

  <div class="start-guidance">

    <h2>A simple practice</h2>

    <p>
      When you notice yourself struggling, pause and ask:
    </p>

    <blockquote>
      What might a kind, wise adult say to me right now?
    </blockquote>

    <p>
      Don't worry about getting it right.
      The goal is not perfection.
      The goal is relationship.
    </p>

  </div>

  <div class="start-soft-block">

    <h2>Before you move on</h2>

    <p>
      Reparenting is less about fixing wounds and more about building trust.
      Trust grows through repetition.
    </p>

    <p>
      Small acts of care repeated consistently become something powerful.
    </p>
