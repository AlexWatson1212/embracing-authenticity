These are not rules.

Most of these ideas were learned slowly — through mistakes, therapy, difficult relationships, grief, parenting, and the repeated discovery that the same patterns often appear in different forms.

I do not see them as beliefs you have to agree with, or standards you have to live up to.

They are simply ideas I return to when life becomes unclear.

If one helps, keep it. If another does not fit your experience, leave it.

A practical philosophy

Seven ideas that support a more authentic life.

These principles are interconnected. You do not need to read them in order. Start wherever feels most relevant today.

A final thought

These principles are not a destination.

They are a way of remaining oriented when life becomes uncertain.

Not a set of answers. Not a standard to perform. More like something to return to when you need to find your footing again.

A reminder that adulthood is not about becoming certain. It is about staying honest, grounded, and intact while living inside the questions.
