I came to the park this morning and ended up staying far longer than I expected. About forty-five minutes of wandering, pausing, exploring, and letting my inner child take the lead.

There’s something about this place that feels different. The pace. The people. The ease. It’s a wealthier area, and I can feel that in the atmosphere — the softness, the spaciousness, the quiet confidence of retired couples or young parents walking slowly with toddlers. It’s mid-morning on a Thursday, so the park is filled with exactly the kind of people who radiate calm without even meaning to.

I’ve crossed paths with a few lovely dogs, exchanged small glances of recognition with their owners, and felt that gentle “I see you / you see me” connection that seems tiny but lands in a deep place inside me. Even these micro-moments of contact soothe something that often feels starved.

Now I’m sitting on a fallen log in the sun, and it’s honestly beautiful.

---

## Imagining myself living here

A thought crossed my mind: *“I’d love to live somewhere like this.”*

And immediately, my brain responded with the old familiar pattern:

- *It’s out of my pay bracket.*  
- *I’ll never be able to afford it.*  
- *The only way I could live here is through inheritance.*  

But then something softened.

Maybe that’s just today’s version of me thinking.  
Maybe the future version — 10 years from now — is someone I haven’t even met yet.

A therapist with a stable client base.  
A confident player earning well from poker.  
A man with savings, equity, and a clear direction.  
Someone who’s healed enough that life has opened up in ways he couldn’t imagine back here on this log.

It doesn’t have to be about inheritance.  
And it doesn’t have to be “impossible.”

It might simply be *not yet.*

---

## The pressure to move on

Even as I write this, I feel a familiar tug:

**“Shouldn’t I be doing something else?  
Going somewhere?  
Eating somewhere?  
Making progress?”**

It’s so ingrained — this sense that being still is suspicious, that enjoying something without purpose is irresponsible, that calm needs justification.

But today, there’s no hurry.

The only real thing I need to do is pick up my son in about five hours.

Everything else is optional.

I could wander more, get a coffee, try a Nando’s I’ve never been to, or sit right here and read a book. I’m not even hungry. The sunshine feels good. The log feels good. This moment feels enough.

Maybe the real work today is simply **letting myself stay in a place that feels nourishing** instead of rushing toward the next distraction.

---

## A glimpse of the life I’m growing toward

Sitting here, I can feel a future version of myself in the distance — not as fantasy, but as possibility. A life with more of this:

- calm  
- spaciousness  
- gentle contact  
- beauty  
- safety  
- belonging  

A life where my nervous system is held by the environment rather than fighting it.

This park might be out of reach today, but the feeling I have here — that warm, grounded, peaceful sense of *“I belong here”* — isn’t out of reach at all.

It’s a direction. A compass. A glimpse of who I’m becoming.

And for now, sitting on this log is enough.
